Equatorial Guinea

01

Sopa de Pescado

Coastal staple

A rich, deeply spiced fish soup simmered with palm oil, tomatoes, and forest herbs. Every coastal family has their own version passed down through generations.Some add plantain, others finish with a squeeze of wild lime.

Seafood

Ingredients

  • Fresh whole fish (tilapia or snapper)
  • Palm oil
  • Ripe tomatoes
  • Onion & garlic
  • Scotch bonnet pepper
  • Forest herbs (bush basil)
  • Plantain (optional)

02

Succotash

Inland villages

A vibrant mix of corn, lima beans, and tomatoes slow-sautéed together until tender and fragrant. In Equatorial Guinea, this dish takes on a West African character enriched with a splash of palm oil and seasoned with local spices, making it heartier and more aromatic than its American counterpart.

Vegetable

Ingredients

  • Sweet corn kernels
  • Lima beans
  • Cherry tomatoes
  • Onion & garlic
  • Palm oil
  • Fresh basil
  • Salt & local spices

Ghana

01

Jollof Rice

Celebration staple

Fragrant long-grain rice slow-cooked in a bold tomato-and-pepper base, often served at celebrations alongside fried plantain and grilled chicken. Every family guards their own recipe fiercely.

Rice Dish

Ingredients

  • Long-grain parboiled rice
  • Tomatoes & red peppers
  • Onion & garlic
  • Tomato paste
  • Chicken stock
  • Bay leaves & thyme
  • Scotch bonnet pepper

02

Fufu & Light Soup

National dish

Pounded cassava and plantain shaped into smooth, stretchy balls, dipped into a delicate palm-nut or groundnut soup with goat or tilapia. Every bite is scooped and swallowed whole tradition insists on it.

National Dish

Ingredients

  • Cassava & unripe plantain
  • Goat meat or tilapia
  • Palm-nut cream
  • Tomatoes & onion
  • Scotch bonnet pepper
  • Garden eggs (optional)

Nigeria

01

Egusi Soup

Throughout Nigeria

Ground melon seeds simmered in palm oil with leafy greens, stockfish, and assorted meats intensely flavourful and served with pounded yam or eba. One of Nigeria's most beloved and widely eaten soups.

Soup

Ingredients

  • Ground egusi (melon seeds)
  • Palm oil
  • Leafy greens (ugu or spinach)
  • Stockfish & smoked fish
  • Assorted meat
  • Crayfish & pepper
  • Onion & seasoning cubes

02

Suya

Street food classic

Skewered, spice-rubbed grilled beef dusted in groundnut powder Nigeria's quintessential street food, smoky and aromatic. Sold at roadside stalls after dark, wrapped in newspaper with sliced onion and tomato.

Street Food

Ingredients

  • Thin-sliced beef (sirloin)
  • Groundnut (peanut) powder
  • Yaji spice blend
  • Ginger & garlic powder
  • Paprika & cayenne
  • Vegetable oil

South Africa

01

Braai & Boerewors

Cultural institution

A social barbecue tradition featuring coiled spiced sausage grilled over open flames more than a meal, it is a cultural institution. Families and friends gather around the fire for hours, sharing stories as the smoke rises.

Grilled

Ingredients

  • Beef & pork mince (boerewors)
  • Coriander seeds
  • Nutmeg & cloves
  • Vinegar & Worcestershire
  • Natural sausage casing
  • Salt & black pepper

02

Bobotie

Cape Malay classic

A Cape Malay classic: curried minced meat baked under a savoury egg custard topping, scented with apricots and turmeric. Warm, aromatic, and unlike anything else — a window into South Africa's layered culinary history.

Baked

Ingredients

  • Minced lamb or beef
  • Curry powder & turmeric
  • Dried apricots
  • Onion & garlic
  • Eggs & milk (custard)
  • Bay leaves & chutney

Cameroon

01

Ndolé

National dish

The national dish: bitter-leaf greens stewed with peanuts, shrimp, and beef or fish in a complex, nutty sauce. Served with plantain or bobolo, it carries the depth and warmth of Central African cooking in every spoonful.

National Dish

Ingredients

  • Bitter leaf (ndolé greens)
  • Ground peanuts
  • Shrimp & beef
  • Crayfish powder
  • Onion, garlic & ginger
  • Palm oil
  • Plantain or bobolo (to serve)

02

Koki Corn

Street & home food

Steamed black-eyed pea or corn cakes wrapped in banana leaves, seasoned with palm oil and spices; a beloved street and home food. The banana leaves impart a faint, grassy fragrance that makes each cake unmistakably Cameroonian.

Steamed

Ingredients

  • Black-eyed peas or corn
  • Palm oil
  • Banana leaves (for wrapping)
  • Crayfish & pepper
  • Salt & local spices

Ethiopia

01

Injera & Doro Wat

National dish

Spongy sourdough flatbread served as both plate and utensil, piled with doro wat a fiery, slow-cooked chicken stew built on the berbere spice blend. Sharing one large injera from a single platter is an act of communal warmth.

National Dish

Ingredients

  • Teff flour (injera)
  • Chicken pieces (bone-in)
  • Berbere spice blend
  • Niter kibbeh (spiced butter)
  • Onion & garlic
  • Hard-boiled eggs

02

Tibs

Ethiopian favourite

Sautéed chunks of lamb or beef stir-fried with onions, rosemary, and chilli in a hot pan crispy-edged, fragrant, and deeply satisfying. Tibs can be mild or fiery, dry or saucy, depending on the region and the cook.

Stir-Fried

Ingredients

  • Lamb or beef chunks
  • Onion & garlic
  • Fresh rosemary
  • Green chilli & jalapeño
  • Niter kibbeh or butter
  • Mitmita or berbere spice
