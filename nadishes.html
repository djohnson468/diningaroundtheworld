01

Stew Fish with Johnny cake

Breakfast · Savory Stew

View full recipe

Ingredients

  • White fish
  • Tomato paste
  • Flour
  • Water
  • Onion (finely chopped)
  • Celery (finely chopped)
  • Fresh thyme
  • Oil
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Hot pepper (optional)
  • Lime juice
  • Orange juice (sour orange if available)
  • Whole allspice
  • Salt
  • Black pepper

Method

  1. Season the fish with garlic, salt, black pepper, and hot pepper, then set aside for a few minutes.
  2. Add lime juice and orange juice to the fish and let it sit to absorb the flavor.
  3. Heat oil in a pan sauté the onion, celery, and thyme until softened, then set aside.
  4. In the same pan, lightly fry the fish on both sides until browned, then remove from pan.
  5. Lower heat and stir in flour, cooking until browned. Gradually add water while stirring until it thickens, then mix in the tomato paste.
  6. Add fish and vegetables back into the pan and let everything simmer until the fish is fully cooked and the sauce is rich and thick. Season to taste and serve with Johnny cake.

Chicken Souse

Lunch / Dinner · Light Soup

View full recipe

Ingredients

  • Chicken wings
  • Lemon or lime
  • Onion
  • Celery stalk
  • Pepper flakes
  • Garlic powder
  • All spice
  • Potatoes, cubed
  • Carrot, sliced

Method

  1. Add rinsed chicken wings to a pot. Squeeze the lemon or lime on top and add salt. Toss well, and cover with fresh water. Bring to a boil and let boil for 8 minutes.
  2. Remove water from pot and rinse the the wings. Cover wings with fresh water, and bring to a boil for 5 minutes. Remove water from pot and rinse wings, again.
  3. Return the wings to the pot and cover with fresh water. Squeeze a lemon or lime, add chopped onion, salt, chopped celery, garlic powder, pepper flakes, and all spice.
  4. Return pot to the stove and allow to boil for 10 minutes. Lower the heat to medium and add potatoes and carrots. Let simmer for 10 minutes or until veggies are soft.
  5. Serve with Johnny cakes or bread.

Benny Cake

Dessert · Sesame Snack

View full recipe

Ingredients

  • Raw benny seeds (also known sesame seeds)
  • Sugar
  • Salt
  • Water

Method

  1. Heat a pot over medium heat and add sesame seeds. Stir constantly while they toast to ensure they don't burn. Once the seeds start lightly popping and become aromatic, remove from the pan.
  2. In the same pot, add sugar and water, and let come to a boil, until it thickens into a syrup. This is your simple syrup.
  3. Add a pinch of salt. Stir in the toasted sesame seeds back into the simple syrup mixture, and mix until everything is evenly coated.
  4. Let the mixture cool for a few minutes. Spoon small portions onto parchment paper, shaping them like rounds or clusters.
  5. Let the candies cool and harden fully, preferably overnight, until they set.

Saltfish and Bakes

Breakfast · Island Breakfast

View full recipe

Ingredients

    Saltfish

  • Saltfish (soaked in water overnight or several days)
  • Onion, diced
  • Garlic, minced
  • Thyme
  • Smoked paprika
  • White pepper
  • Green onions, diced
  • Parsley
  • Tomatoes, diced
  • Hot sauce or hot pepper
  • Optional: Bouillon powder

    • Bakes

  • Flour
  • Shortening or butter
  • Baking powder
  • Sugar
  • Salt
  • Water

Method

  1. Sauté onion, garlic, thyme and paprika for 2-3 minutes, then add tomatoes and cook for an additional 3 minutes. Then add hot sauce, and white pepper. Cook all together for 5 minutes and add water if needed.
  2. Add saltfish to cooked mixture and cook for an additional 5 minutes. Taste and adjust seasoning, if needed, then set aside and let cool.
  3. Mix flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt, then fold in butter or shortening. Add water slowly until a soft dough forms.
  4. Knead lightly, let rest for 15-20 minutes. Then divide into pieces and shape and flatten to your desired shape and size.
  5. Fry in oil for 3-5 minutes or until golden brown, and drain on paper towel. (Or bake at 375°F for 15-18 minutes, or until lightly browned, if baking is preferred cooking method.)
  6. Split bakes open and fill with saltfish. Serve warm.

Fish Cakes

Appetizer · Fried Bites

View full recipe

Ingredients

  • Salt cod, boneless and skinless
  • Onion, diced
  • Flour
  • Water
  • Baking powder
  • Egg, lightly beaten
  • Hot pepper, finely diced
  • Parsley, chopped
  • Thyme, chopped
  • Marjoram, chopped
  • Oil, for frying

Method

  1. Boil saltfish in water for 4 minutes. Drain, add fresh water, and boil again for 3 minutes (this removes extra salt). Drain and flake fish with fork.
  2. In a bowl, combine flaked fish, diced onion, finely diced hot pepper, chopped parsley, chopped thyme, chopped marjoram, lightly beaten egg, flour, baking powder, and water.
  3. Stir until you get a thick batter, add water or flour to get to your desired texture.
  4. Heat oil on medium heat. Drop spoonfuls of batter into oil, flip as needed until golden brown. Do not overcrowd.
  5. Drain on paper towels, serve immediately after frying.

Pudding and Souse

Lunch / Dinner · Local Favorite

View full recipe

Ingredients

    Pudding:

  • Sweet potato, grated
  • Thyme
  • Marjoram
  • Chives, chopped
  • Scotch bonnet pepper, finely diced
  • Sugar
  • Salt
  • Oil
  • Butter
  • Optional: Coloring

    • Souse:

  • Pork shoulder (or traditionally: ear, snout, and tongue), cut into bite-size pieces
  • Large onion, diced
  • Cucumbers, grated
  • Scotch bonnet pepper, finely diced
  • Limes, juiced
  • Fresh parsley, chopped

Method

  1. Boil pork in salted water until it's nice and tender, then let cool.

  4. In a bowl, combine grated sweet potato, thyme, marjoram, chopped chives, finely diced scotch bonnet pepper, sugar, salt, oil, and coloring (if adding). This is your pudding. Mix well.
  5. Either steam or bake pudding in greased pan until it's fully set and cooked through.
  6. Serve cold souse with hot pudding on the side (Optional: serve with a side of sweet potato or pickled breadfruit).

Dessert · Spiced Cake

View full recipe

Ingredients

  • Cassava, peeled and grated (or frozen cassava, grated and thawed)

  • Ground cinnamon
  • Fresh nutmeg, grated
  • Black pepper
  • Vanilla extract
  • Almond extract (or Caribbean mixed essence)
  • Brown sugar
  • Butter, softened
  • Evaporated milk

Method

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F and grease baking pan (use smaller pan for thicker pone and bigger for thinner).

Toutons

Breakfast · Fried Dough

View full recipe

Ingredients

  • White bread dough (store bought or homemade)
  • Oil (for a more traditional taste use rendered pork fat)
  • Flour
  • Optional (for serving): Butter, Molasses

Method

  1. Form white bread dough into balls and flatten into rounds. Let rest on a floured surface for 15-20 minutes to rise.
  2. Heat oil in a large skillet at medium low heat. Place the rounds into the oil and flip when golden to ensure both sides are equally fried.
  3. Place toutons on a parchment lined cookie sheet and place into 250°F oven for about 10 minutes to ensure they are cooked thoroughly.
  4. Serve warm with a pat of butter and drizzled molasses.

Tourtière

Lunch / Dinner · Meat Pie

View full recipe

Ingredients

  • All purpose flour, unbleached
  • Kosher salt
  • butter or lard, chilled
  • Ice cold water
  • Large Russet or Yukon Gold potatoes
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Small onion, finely chopped
  • Garlic clove, finely minced
  • Ground pork
  • Ground beef
  • Poultry seasoning
  • Ground cinnamon
  • Ground cloves
  • Ground nutmeg
  • Ground pepper
  • Egg, scrambled

Method

  1. Make your dough by mixing flour and salt, cutting in cold butter, then adding cold water until it forms. Shape it, wrap it, and chill while you prep everything else.
  2. Boil potatoes until tender, then mash them (save some of the starchy potato water).
  3. Cook onions and garlic in a pan, then add ground beef and ground pork and cook until browned. Add your seasonings and a little potato water, then let it simmer.
  4. Stir the mashed potatoes into the meat mixture and let it cool slightly. Preheat your oven to 400°F.
  5. While the oven is preheating, roll out the dough, place one layer in a pie dish. Add the filling and then cover with the top crust. Seal the edges, brush with egg wash, and cut small vents.
  6. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until golden brown. Then let cool before slicing and serving.

Blueberry Grunt

Dessert · Berry Dessert

View full recipe

Ingredients

  • Blueberries (wild preferred), fresh or frozen
  • Sugar
  • Water
  • Cinnamon (optional)
  • Flour
  • Baking powder
  • Salt
  • Butter (or lard), cold and cubed
  • Milk, cold
  • Optional (for serving): Whipped cream or Vanilla ice cream

Method

  1. Add blueberries, sugar, and a little water to a pot and bring to a simmer until the berries start releasing juices.
  2. Lower the heat and let it cook down slightly until it gets nice and syrupy.
  3. While that's going, mix your dough (flour, baking powder, salt, sugar, cold butter, cold milk) until it forms a soft biscuit dough.
  4. Drop spoonfuls of the dough right on top of the blueberry mixture (do not mix it in).
  5. Cover the pot and let everything cook/steam for 15 minutes or until the dough is fully cooked through and fluffy.
  6. Serve warm. (Optional: top with whipped cream or vanilla ice cream.)

Mangú con Los Tres Golpes

Breakfast · Hearty Breakfast

View full recipe

Ingredients

  • Red onion, thinly sliced
  • White vinegar
  • Frying cheese (queso blanco, also seen as 'queso de freir')
  • Dominican salami (also known as salami especial)
  • Green plantains
  • Salt
  • Salted butter
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Vegetable oil
  • Eggs
  • Salt & pepper

Method

  1. Soak sliced onions in vinegar while you prep everything else.
  2. Boil the plantains in salted water until fork-tender, then drain (save some of the water) and mash with butter until smooth, adding some of the water as needed. This is your mangú.
  3. Fry the cheese until golden, then fry the salami in the same pan and set both aside. Fry eggs to your liking (usually sunny side up).
  4. Sauté the onions with a little oil and vinegar until soft but still slightly crisp.
  5. Plate the mangú, top with onions, and serve with salami, cheese, and eggs on the side.

Chilaquiles

  • Oil

  • Tomatillos

Aguachile

Appetizer · Spicy-Citrusy Seafood

View full recipe

Ingredients

  • Raw shrimp (peeled, deveined, tails removed)
  • Limes
  • Salt
  • Red onion, very thinly sliced
  • White vinegar (optional)
  • Water
  • Garlic clove
  • Fresh cilantro
  • Jalapeños
  • Tortilla chips or mini tostadas (for serving)
  • Optional: Serrano pepper, Cucumber (sliced), Radishes (sliced), Avocado (sliced), Olive oil

Method

  1. Slice shrimp in half lengthwise, remove veins, and place in shallow dish. Cover with lime juice and a pinch of salt. Let the shrimp sit until shrimp turns pink (about 20-30 minutes).
  2. Thinly slice red onion and soak in water with a little salt (and optional vinegar) to mellow the flavor. Set aside.
  3. In a blender, combine lime juice, garlic, cilantro, jalapeños, serrano (optional), and salt. Blend until smooth.
  4. Pour the blended marinade over the shrimp and toss to coat evenly.
  5. Drain the onions and add them to the shrimp. Add cucumber or radish if you want, then chill for about 30 minutes.
  6. Taste and add salt if needed. Then top with avocado, cilantro, and a drizzle of olive oil if desired. Serve chilled with chips or tostadas.

  1. Combine whole milk, evaporated milk, sugar, vanilla extract, and cinammon sticks into a medium saucepan. Stir until well combined. Over medium-low heat allow the mixture to simmer for 8 minutes or until it comes to a gentle boil. Remove from heat and allow to cool.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk the egg and egg yolks until very well combined.
  3. While the milk mixture comes to room temperature, preheat the oven to 350°F. Place individual ramekins into a baking dish or roasting pan and set aside. Remove the cinnamon sticks from the cooled milk mixture. Start boiling water for the water bath.
  4. Temper the eggs by slowly adding and mixing the cooled milk mixture into the eggs. Do this little by little to ensure you do not cook and scramble the eggs. Continue until all of the milk has been thouroughly mixed into the eggs. Pour the mixture into the ramekins.
  5. Carefully pour the bowling water into the baking dish, to cover halfway up the ramekins, not to get any into the jericallas mixture. This cooking method is referred to as baño maria, and is used for cooking custard type of desserts! Place the baking dish into the oven and cook for 30 to 35 minutes, using a toothpick for doneness.

Tejuino

Beverage · Corn Drink

View full recipe

Ingredients

  • Masa harina
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Piloncillo (brown sugar)
  • Limes
  • Optional (for serving): Sea salt, Crushed ice or lime sorbet

Method

  1. Mix masa harina, salt, and warm water and let rest. This is your masa dough.
  2. In a pot, simmer piloncillo with water until fully dissolved, then lower heat.
  3. Blend portions of the masa dough with water until smooth, then slowly stir into the syrup. Repeat until all of the masa and syrup is combined.
  4. Cook the mixture over medium heat, stirring, until it thickens. Remove from heat and mix in fresh lime juice.
  5. Cover and let sit in a cool place for 1-3 days to ferment.
  6. Once ready, blend again if needed, serve over ice add a pinch of sea salt and lime juice. (Optional: top with lime sorbet and lime slice for extra flavor.)

Trini Doubles

Breakfast · Street Food

View full recipe

Ingredients

    For the Bara (fried dough)

  • All-purpose flour
  • Baking powder
  • Salt
  • Instant yeast
  • Tumeric
  • Sugar
  • Water, lukewarm
  • Oil

    • For the Channa (curried chickpeas)

  • Dried chickpeas
  • Baking soda
  • Garlic
  • Bandhania/Culantro (or cilantro)
  • Tumeric
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Optional: Amchar masala or roasted cumin

    • For the Chutneys

    Cucumber Chutney:

  • Cucumber, grated
  • Garlic, minced
  • Hot pepper (to taste)
  • Bandhania/cilantro
  • Salt

    • Green (bandhania) Chutney:

  • Bandhania/culantro
  • Garlic
  • Hot pepper
  • Salt
  • Water (to blend)

Method

  1. In a bowl, mix flour, baking powder, salt, yeast, tumeric, and sugar. Slowly add warm water and mix until you get a soft, slightly sticky dough. Do not overwork it.
  2. Rub a little oil on the dough, cover it, and let it sit for about 1 hour (or until doubled). Then divide into small balls and let them rest again.
  3. Oil your hands and flatten each dough ball into a thin round. Fry in hot oil for a few seconds on each side until puffed and golden. Set aside on paper towels.
  4. Soak chickpeas overnight, then boil with seasoning until tender. Add garlic, bandhania (culantro), tumeric, and spices. Let it simmer and thicken, stirring until it's a nice saucy consistency.
  5. Mix together your cucumber chutney ingredients in a bowl. For the green (bandhania) chutney, blend everything until smooth, adding a little water if needed.
  6. Take one or two baras, add a scoop of chickpeas, then top with chutneys. Serve warm.

Pelau

Lunch / Dinner · One-Pot Rice

View full recipe

Ingredients

  • Chicken thighs, bone-in, skin-on
  • Thyme (fresh or dried)
  • Garlic cloves, minced
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Vegetable oil
  • Brown sugar
  • Onion, chopped
  • Carrot, diced
  • Dry pigeon peas, rinsed and drained
  • Long-grain parboiled rice, washed and drained
  • Cilantro, finely chopped
  • Green onion, thinly sliced
  • Coconut milk
  • Low-sodium chicken stock (or hot water)
  • Optional: Scotch bonnet pepper, Golden Ray margarine

Method

  1. Throw the chicken in a bowl with thyme, garlic, salt, and pepper. Mix well. (Optional: Let marinate overnight for more flavor.)
  2. Heat oil in a big pot, then sprinkle in brown sugar and let melt down until it turns into a deep brown. Do not let burn.
  3. Carefully add the chicken to the pot and let it cook in the carmelized sugar. Stir and cook until it's nicely browned on all sides.
  4. Throw in your onion, carrot, and pigeon peas. Let that cook a bit, then add the rice and mix everything together. Add cilantro, green onion, thyme, and whole scotch bonnet pepper.
  5. Pour in coconut milk and chicken stock, scrape the bottom of the pot to get all of the flavor. Season a little more, then cover and let cook on low until the rice is tender and everything absorbs.
  6. Take it off heat, remove thyme (if using fresh), and add a little pat of butter. Fluff up rice, and then serve hot or room temperature.
